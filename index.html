TOP PICK: Obudu Mountain Resort

🏔️ Obudu, Cross River

Escape to the clouds at Obudu! With its spectacular and scenic views, the mountain resort capitalizes on its altitude to offer guests invigorating mountain air and breathtaking viewpoints. Key highlights include a stunning grotto featuring a natural swimming pool with crystal-clear water and a view of winding waterfalls nestled among lush green mountain ranges. The resort also boasts cable car rides, rolling green hills, canopy walkways, and a wildlife reserve home to rare species such as gorillas, drill monkeys, and exotic birds. Obudu Mountain Resort is a highland adventure perfect for both thrill-seekers and nature lovers alike.

Must Visit Destinations

💦 Suleja, Niger State

Gurara Waterfalls

Discover the raw power and beauty of nature at Gurara Waterfalls, one of Nigeria’s most spectacular cascades. With thunderous waters plunging over rocky cliffs into serene pools, it’s a perfect spot for picnics, photography, and quiet moments by nature’s side. During the dry season, the calm pools invite swimming and exploration, while the surrounding landscape offers panoramic views of flowering trees and lush greenery. Whether you're an adventurer or a romantic soul, Gurara is a natural marvel you won’t forget.

🖼️ Ibadan, Oyo State

Topfat Art Gallery

Uncover the soul of Nigerian creativity at Topfat Art Gallery in Ibadan. This artistic haven showcases a rich tapestry of contemporary and traditional African art—from bold paintings and intricate sculptures to cultural artifacts. Whether you're an art enthusiast or a curious traveler, the gallery offers a window into the vibrant spirit of Nigerian expression, with rotating exhibitions and pieces available for collectors and appreciators alike.

🏰 Kajuru, Kaduna State

Kajuru Castle

Hidden within the hills of Kaduna lies Kajuru Castle, a medieval-style fortress that feels like a step into a literal fairy tale. This stone-built structure offers breathtaking views, romantic turrets, spiral staircases, and even a drawbridge. Guests can swim in the castle’s private pool, explore the castle’s unique rooms and dungeons, and enjoy rooftop dinners overlooking the hills. Whether you're seeking royal luxury or a one-of-a-kind retreat, Kajuru Castle delivers a magical, storybook experience.

⚓ Badagry, Lagos State

Badagry Slave Museum and Black History Museum

Step back into history at the Badagry Slave Museum and Black History Museum, where powerful exhibits tell the Nigerian history of the transatlantic slave trade era. Located along the historic slave route, the museum features relics, chains, and firsthand accounts that honor the memories of those who passed through Badagry. It's a deeply moving and educational destination, offering insight, remembrance, and a better understanding of Africa’s historical journey.

🐘 Bauchi State

Yankari Game Reserve

Go wild at Yankari Game Reserve, Nigeria’s premier wildlife park. This vast savannah is home to elephants, baboons, antelopes, and more, freely roaming the land. Safari tours bring you up close with nature, while the crystal-clear Wikki Warm Springs offer a relaxing, mineral-rich swim surrounded by tropical forest. With caves to explore and nature trails to trek, Yankari promises a thrilling and immersive wildlife experience.

🌳 Lekki, Lagos State

Lekki Conservation Centre

Adventure meets conservation at the Lekki Conservation Centre, a lush urban sanctuary just outside Lagos. The reserve is famous for Africa’s longest canopy walkway, giving visitors a thrilling bird’s-eye view of the forest. It’s also home to a variety of wildlife including monkeys, crocodiles, and peacocks. Wooden boardwalks guide you through mangroves and swamps, while picnic spots and an educational center make it great for families. It’s the perfect escape into nature within the city.

🪨 Near Madalla, Niger State (Abuja outskirts)

Zuma Rock

Meet the majestic Zuma Rock, a towering natural monolith standing sentinel at Abuja’s gate. Known as the “Gateway to the Capital,” this enormous rock features a mysterious human-like face etched by nature. The site is surrounded by myth, legend, and panoramic landscapes, making it a must-see for hikers, photographers, and folklore lovers. It's dramatic height and cultural significance make Zuma Rock a proud symbol of Nigeria’s natural wonders.

🎶 Lagos Island, Lagos State

Freedom Park

From prison walls to cultural heartbeat, Freedom Park has transformed into one of Lagos’ most vibrant gathering spaces. Once a colonial-era prison, it now pulses with music, art, festivals, and food. Visitors can relax in the open-air theatre, explore the historical museum, and join locals for evening performances and concerts. It’s where history, creativity, and community come alive in the heart of the city.

🏞️ Ado Awaye, Oyo State

Ado Awaye Mountains and Suspended Lakes

Hidden in the mountains of Oyo lies one of the world’s rarest natural wonders — the Iyake Suspended Lake atop the Ado Awaye Hills. This mystical body of water appears to float above the landscape, surrounded by ancient caves, elephant-shaped rock formations, and spiritual legends. The challenging hike to the top rewards adventurers with unforgettable views and a surreal connection to nature’s magic. It's a bucket-list destination for hikers and explorers alike.
